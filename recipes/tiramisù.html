Tiramisù

Description

Tiramisù is an Italian dessert consisting of ladyfingers soaked in espresso, layered with mascarpone cheese whipped to lightness, then dusted with cocoa powder.

Ingridients

  • 16 espresso coffee shots
  • 24 savoiardi (ladyfinger cookies)
  • 2 cups mascarpone cheese at room temperature
  • 4 large eggs
  • 3/4 cup granulated sugar, divided
  • Cocoa powder for dusting

Steps

  1. In a shallow dish, combine espresso coffee shots with granulated sugar to make a syrup.
  2. Dip half of the savoiardi in the coffee syrup until they are soaked, but not soggy.
  3. Spread mascarpone cheese on the soaked ladyfingers and arrange them in a baking dish or individual glasses.
  4. Beat eggs with remaining sugar and pour over the mascarpone-limited layer.
  5. Repeat the layering process until all ingredients are used, ending with a layer of mascarpone and espresso-soaked ladyfingers.
  6. Refrigerate for at least 6 hours before serving to allow it to set-up properly.
  7. Dust cocoa powder on top right before serving.
